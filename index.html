Philippines — Literacy Crisis (Key Facts)

A concise, single-page summary of the latest findings on literacy and learning outcomes in the Philippines.

Scale of the Problem

24 million Filipinos aged 10–64 are functionally illiterate.

Among them, 5.8 million are basically illiterate (cannot read or write at basic levels).

High School Graduates (2024)

About 1 in 5 of 2024 high school graduates remained functionally illiterate; over 18.9 million individuals struggle with reading comprehension, writing and basic math.

Children & Learning Poverty

World Bank findings indicate around 91% of 10-year-old Filipino children cannot comprehend simple written text — a situation worsened by the pandemic.

Context & Assessment Results

Educational standards were already declining prior to COVID-19. In the 2022 PISA assessment, Filipino 15-year-olds scored 14 points in the creative thinking domain (out of 60), placing the country among the lowest performers in the 2022 cycle. This assessment evaluates students' ability for imaginative problem-solving and innovative thinking.

Why this matters

Low literacy and poor creative problem-solving skills limit individuals' employment opportunities, reduce national productivity, and make socioeconomic recovery from shocks (like the pandemic) much harder. Addressing foundational learning and teacher support is crucial.

Key numbers (at-a-glance)

  • 24,000,000 — Estimated functionally illiterate (ages 10–64)
  • 5,800,000 — Basically illiterate
  • ~18,900,000 — High-school graduates (2024) struggling with basic skills
  • 91% — 10-year-olds not proficient in reading (World Bank learning poverty estimate)
  • 14 / 60 — Mean PISA creative thinking score for Filipino 15-year-olds (2022)

Suggested actions

  1. Prioritise early-grade reading programs and remediation.
  2. Invest in teacher training focused on foundational literacy techniques.
  3. Target out-of-school and at-risk children with accelerated learning modules.
  4. Strengthen monitoring with periodic, comparable assessments to guide policy.
